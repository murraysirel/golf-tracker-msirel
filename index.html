Game Modes — How to Play
Match Play
Players & Teams

Any number from 2–4 players. Select your format on the next screen: singles (1v1), pairs (2v2), or an uneven 2v1. Assign players to Team A and Team B before the round starts.

Winning Holes

Each hole is won, lost, or halved. In pairs or 2v1, the best net score from each side wins the hole. Net score = gross score minus any handicap strokes received on that hole based on stroke index.

Winning the Match
Win a hole → go 1UP
Lose a hole → go 1DOWN
Halve a hole → no change
Lead by more holes than remain → win by X&Y
After 18 holes still tied → match halved
Dormie

When one side leads by the same number of holes that remain, they are dormie — the trailing side must win every remaining hole just to halve.

Sixes (3-ball)
The Format

Exactly 3 players. The round is split into three 6-hole matches — holes 1–6, 7–12, and 13–18. In each block the pairings rotate so each player partners with each other player once.

Points Per Hole
Pair wins hole → 4 pts to pair, 0 to solo
Solo wins hole → 2 pts to solo, 0 each to pair
Pair wins & one halves → 4–1–1
All tie → 2–2–2
Winner

Most points after 18 holes wins. Played on net scores (handicap strokes applied).

Wolf (4-player)
The Order

Throw a ball at the tee marker — closest to furthest sets playing order. Enter it in the app before play starts.

Each Hole

The Wolf rotates each hole and tees off first. After each opponent's tee shot, the Wolf may call "Partner!" before the next player hits. Pass all three and the Wolf plays Lone Wolf.

Points (no carry on ties)
2v2 — Wolf's pair wins → +2 pts each
2v2 — Other pair wins → +3 pts each
Lone Wolf wins → Wolf +3 pts
Lone Wolf loses → opponents +1 pt each
Any tie → no points change
The 6-Pointer (once per round)

Before anyone tees off, the Wolf may stake everything. Win → Wolf +6 pts. Lose → opponents +3 pts each. Each player may only do this once.

Holes 17 & 18

The player in second place becomes Wolf on 17. Recalculate after 17 — that player is Wolf on 18. Ties go to whoever is earliest in the original order.
